### Amino acid Catabolism
- Catabolism of amino acids generates ammonia and a carbon skeleton
- Glu + H2O $\to$ NH4+ + $\alpha$-ketoglutarate
- Carbon skeleton catabolism can either generate glucose or acetyl-CoA
- Ammonia is toxic in elevated concentrations and must be disposed of 
	- NH3 direct disposal (fish)
	- Urea (mammals)
	- Uric acid (reptiles, birds)
- The urea cycle is how mammals synthesize urea for disposal

### Urea Cycle
- The entire cycle requires 4 ATP equivalents
