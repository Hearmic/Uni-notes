## Isozymes
- Enzymes that differ in amino acid sequence but catalyze the same chemical reaction
- Convergent Evolution: Converged to run the same reaction

### Cyclooxygenase (Cox 1 and Cox 2)
- Inhibit cotrel inflammation
- NSAIDS - non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs 
- 65% homology (nearly identical catalytic sites)
- Cox1: Regulation of muscle contraction / G1 track mucous production
- Ibuprofen is a nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID) used to reduce fever and treat mild to moderate pain, inflammation, arthritis, and menstrual cramps. Commonly branded as Advil or Motrin, it works by inhibiting [cyclooxygenase enzymes](https://www.google.com/search?sca_esv=484202b0491193e0&rlz=1C5CHFA_enKR1054KR1054&sxsrf=ANbL-n5CkerExq7zHXEqaO5NbVA-X0f6lQ%3A1774446644328&q=cyclooxygenase+enzymes&source=lnms&fbs=ADc_l-ZhSlbfqeZsLDJhbRo02Bk6hPEHQhiM_EOk6El4D3oe4aUwNdLQvE3eUaGmOSQVGzwJZexiiwC6WNtM28lEqsv6SFCAXbkD4OEHw6h5F5gd5nFY_Ug0bPs0mO6KERXHsHAG3Gv9tQRP3rWWkk3vDwrQ8rYg3x0YazT5WTVTZm-MTrDJ0S31e11jMk2RoSZBZKs-dpQVyNU_UI1mlXk4joxferFyR14KY2VLvjNU7HDjTaZwOZs&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiB0ZXwmLuTAxW6LTQIHdoVLnYQgK4QegYIAQgAEAY&biw=1440&bih=781&dpr=2.5) (COX-1 and COX-2) to block prostaglandin production
